## üé® 2. COLOR PALETTE

### Primary Palette

#### Cosmic Blues & Purples
- **Deep Indigo**: `#1a1a3e` - Space backgrounds, shadows
- **Royal Purple**: `#6b46c1` - Magical accents, energy
- **Electric Blue**: `#3b82f6` - Bioluminescence, glows
- **Cyan**: `#06b6d4` - Water, ice, crystal effects
- **Lavender**: `#a78bfa` - Soft magical auras

#### Warm Cosmic Accents
- **Fuchsia Pink**: `#ec4899` - Energy, magical effects
- **Golden Yellow**: `#fbbf24` - Celestial orbs, warm glows
- **Orange-Red**: `#f97316` - Planets, warm lighting
- **Coral**: `#fb7185` - Soft accents, flora

#### Neutrals & Base Colors
- **Dark Grey**: `#1f2937` - Structures, shadows
- **Light Grey**: `#9ca3af` - Stone, neutral surfaces
- **Cream/Beige**: `#fef3c7` - Fur base colors
- **White**: `#ffffff` - Highlights, stars

### Color Usage Rules

1. **Bioluminescence**: Always use Electric Blue (`#3b82f6`) or Fuchsia Pink (`#ec4899`)
2. **Magical Effects**: Royal Purple (`#6b46c1`) or Lavender (`#a78bfa`)
3. **Energy Sources**: Golden Yellow (`#fbbf24`) or Cyan (`#06b6d4`)
4. **Backgrounds**: Deep Indigo (`#1a1a3e`) with star particles
5. **Accents**: Use warm colors sparingly for contrast

### Color Combinations

**Primary Combinations:**
- Electric Blue + Fuchsia Pink (high contrast, magical)
- Royal Purple + Golden Yellow (warm/cool balance)
- Deep Indigo + Cyan (space atmosphere)
- Lavender + Coral (soft, dreamy)

**Avoid:**
- Pure black backgrounds (use Deep Indigo)
- Muddy browns (use warm greys instead)
- Saturated greens (reserved for foliage only)
- Pure white glows (use soft cyan or pink)

## üé® 2. MODELING STYLE (Meshy Outputs)

### 2.1 Shape Language

**Body Proportions:**
- Use **smooth curves** for the body
- Use **sharp crystalline shards** only as accents (not main body)
- **Tail** should be expressive, slightly longer than normal cats
- **Ears** a bit larger (more expressive silhouette)
- Maintain cute anthropomorphic proportions
- Clean, readable silhouettes

**Design Principles:**
- Stylized, not realistic
- Soft, flowing forms
- Crystalline elements as decorative accents
- Expressive, exaggerated features for readability

### 2.2 Polycount

**Character Models:**
- **5k‚Äì15k polys per character** is appropriate
- NO extreme realism
- NO tiny, noisy geometry
- Optimize for Roblox performance
- Balance detail with performance

**Other Assets:**
- **Structures**: 2k-5k polys (depending on size)
- **Accessories**: 500-2k polys
- **Effects**: 100-1k polys
- **Environment**: Use Roblox Terrain where possible

### 2.3 Materials

**Keep materials simple for Roblox:**

- ‚úî **One skinned mesh** (for characters)
- ‚úî **One material** with well-made texture
- ‚úî **Optional secondary overlay** for glows (SurfaceAppearance)

**Material Guidelines:**
- Use Roblox Standard materials for base
- Use Neon or ForceField for glowing elements
- Use SurfaceAppearance for complex textures
- Avoid multiple material IDs when possible
- Keep texture count minimal

### 2.4 Mesh Cleanliness

**Before import to Roblox Studio:**

- [ ] No overlapping verts
- [ ] No duplicate geometry
- [ ] No floating tiny mesh details
- [ ] Clean topology
- [ ] Proper edge flow
- [ ] No non-manifold geometry
- [ ] All faces properly oriented

**Cursor will handle:**
- Rig & animation connections
- Scale application
- Material assignment
- Attachment points

## üñºÔ∏è 3. TEXTURE STYLE

### 3.1 Texture Principles

Textures should follow:

- ‚úî **Soft gradient body tones** - Smooth color transitions
- ‚úî **Bright glowing accents** - High contrast glowing areas
- ‚úî **Nebula-like dissolves or speckles** - Cosmic texture patterns
- ‚úî **Pattern masks** for creator options - Separate pattern layers
- ‚úî **Clean edges** (no up-close grunge) - Stylized, not photorealistic

### 3.2 Texture Palette Values

**Primary Colors:**
- **Purple**: `#8A4FFF` - Main body tones, magical elements
- **Blue**: `#4F7BFF` - Secondary tones, water, ice
- **Pink**: `#FF8AF2` - Accents, energy, magical effects
- **Cyan**: `#77E3FF` - Highlights, glows, bioluminescence

**Dark Colors:**
- **Deep Purple**: `#1A0F33` - Shadows, deep areas
- **Void Black**: `#0A0020` - Deepest shadows, space

**Glow Colors:**
- **Cyan Glow**: `#C7F8FF` - Bright bioluminescent glows
- **Pink Glow**: `#FFDDFA` - Warm magical glows

### 3.3 Texture Guidelines

**Body Textures:**
- Soft gradients from light to dark
- Nebula-like speckles or dissolves
- Pattern masks for customization
- Clean, stylized edges
- No photorealistic fur detail

**Glowing Elements:**
- High contrast bright areas
- Soft bloom edges
- Nebula-inspired patterns
- Bright accent colors

**Pattern Masks:**
- Separate layer for patterns
- Clean, readable patterns
- Support for multiple pattern types
- Easy to swap in Character Creator

### 3.4 What to Avoid

**Colors:**
- ‚úò Browns (use warm greys instead)
- ‚úò Muddy greens (use vibrant greens only for foliage)
- ‚úò Realistic fur patterns (use stylized patterns)

**Textures:**
- ‚úò Photorealistic detail
- ‚úò Grunge or noise textures
- ‚úò Harsh edges or seams
- ‚úò Overly complex patterns
- ‚úò Realistic material properties

**Style:**
- ‚úò Realistic proportions
- ‚úò Gritty textures
- ‚úò Horror elements
- ‚úò High-detail realism

### 3.5 Texture Resolution

**Character Textures:**
- **Base Texture**: 2048x2048 (preferred) or 1024x1024 (minimum)
- **Pattern Masks**: 1024x1024
- **Emissive Maps**: 1024x1024
- **Normal Maps**: Optional, 1024x1024 if used

**Environment Textures:**
- **Large Structures**: 2048x2048
- **Medium Objects**: 1024x1024
- **Small Details**: 512x512

**Optimization:**
- Use appropriate resolution for object size
- Compress textures moderately
- Maintain quality for glowing elements
- Use texture atlases when possible

### 6.1 Particle Style

**Core Principles:**
- ‚úî **Soft glow bloom** - Gentle, diffused light effects
- ‚úî **Slow-floating shards** - Gentle, drifting crystalline particles
- ‚úî **Star motes** - Small, twinkling star-like particles
- ‚úî **Dust-like movement** - Organic, floating particle motion
- ‚úî **No harsh sparks** - Avoid sharp, aggressive particle effects
- ‚úî **No realistic smoke** - Stylized, not photorealistic

**Particle Characteristics:**
- Soft, ethereal appearance
- Slow, graceful movement
- Organic, floating motion
- Gentle, diffused glows
- Stylized, not realistic
- Magical, cosmic feel

**What to Avoid:**
- ‚úò Harsh, sharp sparks
- ‚úò Realistic smoke or fire
- ‚úò Aggressive particle bursts
- ‚úò Fast, chaotic movement
- ‚úò Photorealistic effects
- ‚úò Gritty or dark effects

- Soft glow
- Sparse to medium density
- Use in: All biomes, background effects

**Aurora Ribbons:**
- Tundra-specific effect
- Flowing, ribbon-like particles
- Cyan, blue, purple colors
- Smooth, wave-like motion
- Soft glow
- Use in: Aurora Tundra biome

**Crystal Glows:**
- Cave-specific effects
- Bright, focused glows
- Cyan, blue, teal colors
- Point light sources
- Refraction effects
- Use in: Lunar Caverns, cave areas

**Orbiting Particles:**
- Premium species effect
- Particles that orbit around body
- Slow, circular motion
- Species-specific colors
- Soft glow
- Use in: Category C and D species

**Additional VFX Elements:**
- **Energy Beams**: Soft, flowing energy streams
- **Bioluminescent Trails**: Glowing particle trails
- **Cosmic Dust**: Floating, twinkling particles
- **Magical Auras**: Soft, pulsing energy fields
- **Portal Effects**: Swirling, ethereal portals

### 6.3 Colored Glows

**Species Abilities Use:**
- **Additive glow** - Bright, luminous glow effect
- **Soft circular masks** - Gentle, rounded glow shapes
- **Slight blur** - Soft edges, not sharp

**Glow Specifications:**
- **Intensity**: Medium to high (species-dependent)
- **Color**: Species-specific (matches bioluminescence)
- **Shape**: Soft, circular, organic
- **Edge**: Blurred, diffused
- **Animation**: Gentle pulsing or slow fade
- **Blend Mode**: Additive for maximum glow

**Glow Colors by Category:**
- **Category A**: Soft cyan, pink, blue (gentle glows)
- **Category B**: Element-specific (moon = cyan, sun = gold)
- **Category C**: High contrast (bright cyan, pink, white)
- **Category D**: Maximum intensity (white, gold, bright colors)

**Glow Implementation:**
- Use PointLights for 3D glow
- Use SurfaceAppearance for emissive materials
- Use particle effects for aura glows
- Use blur effects for soft edges
- Use additive blending for intensity

**Ability VFX Examples:**
- **ZERO_G_HOVER**: Soft energy particles beneath feet
- **GLOW_EYES**: Bright eye glow with soft bloom
- **NEBULA_AURA**: Swirling nebula particles around body
- **STAR_TRAIL**: Twinkling star particles in motion
- **ENERGY_BEAM**: Soft, flowing energy stream

### 6.4 VFX Performance Guidelines

**Optimization:**
- Limit particle count per effect (50-200 particles)
- Use efficient particle systems
- Combine effects when possible
- Use LOD (Level of Detail) for distant effects
- Cache particle effects for reuse

**Quality Settings:**
- **Low**: Reduced particle count, simpler effects
- **Medium**: Standard particle count, full effects
- **High**: Maximum particle count, enhanced effects

**Best Practices:**
- Test VFX in-game lighting conditions
- Ensure visibility in all biomes
- Maintain performance across devices
- Use appropriate particle lifetimes
- Clean up particles properly
